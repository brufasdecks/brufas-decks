The Tenth Doctor and Rose Tyler are a Jeskai partner pair built around time counters and the new time travel mechanic. The Tenth Doctor attacks to exile cards from the top of your library, giving them suspend — placing three time counters on them so they cast for free as the counters tick down. His Timey-Wimey ability lets you time travel three times for {7}, adding or removing time counters from any suspended card or permanent you control. Rose Tyler gets +1/+1 for each time counter on her — and her Bad Wolf ability adds a counter on her for every suspended card you own and every permanent with a time counter whenever she attacks, turning the board state into a massive power boost. Clockspinning, Wibbly-wobbly Timey-wimey, and Inspiring Refrain all manipulate time counters cheaply. As Foretold lets you cast a spell for free each turn based on the number of time counters on it. Sally Sparrow and Idris, Soul of the TARDIS accelerate your suspended cards to the battlefield faster. Everybody Lives! protects your board in emergencies. The whole deck snowballs: more suspended cards mean a bigger Rose, which means more attacks, which means more suspensions.

⚓
Early Game
  • Develop Jeskai mana through your artifact rocks and dual lands — you need White for Rose Tyler and Blue/Red for The Tenth Doctor's suspend engine.
  • Cast Rose Tyler on turn two and attack with her immediately — even a single suspended card or time-counter permanent starts building her power.
  • Cast The Tenth Doctor on turn five and attack to start exiling and suspending cards from your library for free future casts.
⚔️
Midgame
  • Use Clockspinning, Wibbly-wobbly Timey-wimey, and the Doctor's Timey-Wimey ability to remove time counters from suspended cards, accelerating them to the battlefield ahead of schedule.
  • Attack with Rose Tyler repeatedly — each attack stacks time counters on her equal to all suspended cards and time-counter permanents, rapidly growing her into a massive threat.
  • Deploy As Foretold and let its time counters accumulate — each upkeep it gains a counter, letting you cast progressively more expensive spells for free each turn.
💀
Late Game
  • Activate The Tenth Doctor's Timey-Wimey ability to time travel three times in one activation — strip counters from suspended threats to dump them all onto the battlefield simultaneously.
  • Swing with Rose Tyler after building a large time-counter board state — she can reach 10+ power with enough suspended cards and time-counter permanents in play.
  • Use Everybody Lives! to protect your board through mass removal, then untap and swing for lethal with your counter-stacked team.
Plan A

Suspend multiple high-impact cards via The Tenth Doctor's attack trigger, then time travel to remove their counters simultaneously and flood the battlefield with free threats.

Plan B

Stack time counters on Rose Tyler through repeated attacks and the Bad Wolf trigger until she becomes a lethal attacker, then close through commander damage.

Plan C

Assemble As Foretold with multiple time counters to cast a free spell each turn while deploying the full Doctor companion roster for a legendary creature beatdown.

  1. The Tenth Doctor exiles and suspends cards from your library — any card exiled this way that doesn't normally have suspend gains it, so it will cast for free when its counters reach zero.
  2. Rose Tyler's Bad Wolf trigger fires on attack — attack every turn to keep stacking time counters and growing her power, even if she isn't dealing damage.
  3. Time travel lets you add OR remove counters from each permanent — use it to speed up suspended cards by removing counters, or slow down vanishing permanents by adding them.
  4. As Foretold gains one time counter each upkeep — the free cast it enables matches the number of counters on it, so patience makes it increasingly powerful.
  5. Everybody Lives! prevents all life loss and all win/loss conditions for a turn — use it to survive an opponent's lethal swing, but remember players can still concede.
Turn Structure
  1. 1Untap — Untap your permanents.
  2. 2Upkeep — Resolve "at the beginning of your upkeep" effects.
  3. 3Draw — Draw one card.
  4. 4First Main — Play a land, cast spells, and set up attacks.
  5. 5Combat — Choose attackers, resolve blocks, assign damage, and apply combat effects.
  6. 6Second Main — Cast more spells or develop your board after combat.
  7. 7End Step — Resolve "at the beginning of your end step" effects.
  8. 8Cleanup — Discard to hand size and remove damage from creatures.
Common Terms for This Deck
  • Suspend:Cast a card for its suspend cost to exile it with time counters — at the start of each upkeep, remove one counter, and cast it for free when the last counter is removed.
  • Time travel:Add or remove a time counter from each suspended card you own and each permanent you control with a time counter — The Tenth Doctor can do this three times per activation.
  • Bad Wolf:Rose Tyler's named triggered ability that adds time counters on her equal to your total suspended cards and time-counter permanents whenever she attacks.
  • Vanishing:A permanent enters with time counters and is sacrificed when the last one is removed — time travel can add counters to keep vanishing permanents alive longer.
  • Doctor's companion:A keyword that lets a specific creature serve as a second commander alongside the Doctor — Rose Tyler specifically requires The Tenth Doctor as her partner.

Matchups vary by deck, table, and power level — this is a helpful rule of thumb, not a hard rule.
